WSVC

With the development of wireless networks and mobile technologies, there is a growing demand for wireless video transmission. In a limited bandwidth, how to transmit more information while providing a better service is an important work for video coding. In wireless video transmission, signal is sent to different receiving ends under different channel quality, which makes scalable video coding technology applicable. Digital coding schemes has high coding efficiency, but low quality scalability; while analog coding schemes has high quality scalability, but low coding efficiency. In our work, we use a hybrid coding method, which is named as wireless scalable video coding (WSVC), to combine the advantages of digital schemes and analog schemes. WSVC has good coding efficiency and can adapt to different channel quality.

SORA

Software radio (or software-defined radio, SDR) is an engineering pursue in wireless communication technology field that one day all wireless signal processing functions, which are typically implemented in hardware today, will be done completely in software. Over the years, researchers and engineers have made remarkable progress and have been constantly pushing the hardware-software boundary. The Microsoft Research Software Radio (Sora) is one such recent success in advancing the state of the art in software radio technology.

SIPS

The Session Initiation Protocol (SIP) was standardized by the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF) and is described in several documents known as Request for Comment (RFC). RFC3261 is one of the most recent of the documents, and is called SIP version 2. SIP is an application layer protocol used to establish, modify, and terminate sessions or multimedia calls. These sessions can be audio and video sessions, e-learning, chatting, or screen-sharing sessions. It is based on a text protocol similar to Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) and is designed to start, keep, and close interactive communication sessions between users. These days, SIP is one of the most used protocols for VoIP and is present on almost every IP phone in the market
